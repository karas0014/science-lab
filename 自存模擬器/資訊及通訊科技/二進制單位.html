為何 1 KB = 1024 而非 1000？ 電腦以二進制運算，2¹⁰ = 1024 最接近 1000。作業系統多用 1024,而硬碟廠商用 SI 的 1000，所以「1 TB 硬碟」在 Windows 只顯示約 931 GB。國際標準以 KiB/MiB/GiB (二進制) 區別於 KB/MB/GB (十進制)。 Why is 1 KB = 1024, not 1000? Computers work in binary, and 2¹⁰ = 1024 is closest to 1000. OSes mostly use 1024 while drive makers use SI's 1000 — so a "1 TB" disk shows as about 931 GB in Windows. The standard uses KiB/MiB/GiB (binary) to distinguish from KB/MB/GB (decimal).
